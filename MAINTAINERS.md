## Maintainer Expectations

### Time Commitment

Maintainers should expect to dedicate approximately **4–6 hours per week** during active development periods. This may vary based on project activity and release cycles.

### Responsibilities

1. **Issue Triage**
   - Review incoming issues weekly
   - Apply appropriate labels per the triage taxonomy
   - Request clarification when issues lack detail
   - Close stale or out-of-scope issues with explanation

2. **Pull Request Review**
   - Provide first review within 5 business days
   - Give constructive, actionable feedback
   - Approve and merge PRs that meet quality standards
   - Request changes with clear guidance

3. **Security Response**
   - Participate in security triage when vulnerabilities are reported
   - Follow the [Security Response Playbook](.github/SECURITY_RESPONSE_PLAYBOOK.md)
   - Maintain confidentiality until coordinated disclosure

4. **Documentation**
   - Keep documentation current with code changes
   - Review and approve ADRs for significant decisions
   - Update CHANGELOG.md for releases

5. **Release Management**
   - Participate in release preparation
   - Verify release artifacts and documentation
   - Monitor post-release stability

6. **Community Engagement**
   - Respond to questions in Discussions (if enabled)
   - Welcome new contributors
   - Mentor contributors on project conventions

### Decision Making

- Day-to-day decisions are made by individual maintainers
- Significant changes require consensus among maintainers
- Architectural decisions require an ADR in `docs/adr/`
- If consensus cannot be reached, the Lead Maintainer decides

## Becoming a Maintainer

Contributors may be invited to become maintainers after demonstrating:

1. **Sustained Contributions** — A history of high-quality PRs over 3+ months
2. **Reliable Reviews** — Thoughtful, constructive feedback on others' PRs
3. **Follow-Through** — Completing what they start, responding to feedback
4. **Alignment** — Understanding of project goals and scope
5. **Sponsorship** — Nomination by an existing maintainer

### Onboarding Process

1. Existing maintainer nominates candidate
2. Other maintainers review contribution history
3. Consensus approval from current maintainers
4. New maintainer is added to this file and granted repository access
5. Onboarding call/session to cover workflows, access, and expectations

## Stepping Down

Maintainers may step down at any time by notifying other maintainers. We appreciate advance notice when possible to ensure smooth transition.

### Emeritus Status

Maintainers who step down after significant contributions may be listed as Emeritus Maintainers. Emeritus maintainers:

- Are recognized for past contributions
- Do not have active commit or merge access
- May be invited back as active maintainers in the future

### Inactivity

If a maintainer is inactive for 3+ months without notice, remaining maintainers may:

1. Attempt to contact the inactive maintainer
2. After 2 weeks without response, reassign their responsibilities
3. Move them to Emeritus status
